Chocolate Chip Cannolis

Chocolate Chip Cannolis, or Cannolis Con Chispas De Coco; flaky tube of Italian pastry filled with sweet, creamy ricotta cheese and tiny chocolate chips, this traditional treat combines a variety of textures and flavors into one rich dessert.

Ingredients

Cannoli Filling

  • 2 pounds (32 ounces) whole milk ricotta cheese
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ½ teaspoon ground cinnamon (optional)
  • ½ cup mini chocolate chips
  • Garnish: mini chocolate chips, chopped pistachios, or orange zest
  • Garnish: powdered sugar (for sprinkilng)

Cannoli Shells

  • 2 cups plain flour
  • ¾ tablespoons granulated sugar
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, chilled and diced
  • ½ cup Marsala wine (or 1 tablespoon white grape juice and vinegar for non-alcoholic options)
  • 1 large egg (yolk and white separated)
  • Vegetable oil (for frying)

Instructions

For the Filling

  1. Place the ricotta in a fine-mesh strainer lined with cheesecloth. Place the strainer over a bowl, cover, and refrigerate for 8 hours to remove excess moisture.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the drained ricotta, powdered sugar, vanilla extract, and cinnamon until smooth.
  3. Gently fold mini chocolate chips into ricotta mixture.
  4. Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour to allow the filling to set.

For the Shell

  1. Prepare the dough: In a food processor or large bowl, pulse or whisk together the flour, granulated sugar, and salt. Add the cold butter and mix until pebbled.
  2. Mix the liquid ingredients: Add the Marsala wine and egg yolks (reserving the eggs whites to seal). Pulse or mix with a mixer until the dough begins to come together.
  3. Knead and rest: Turn the dough out onto a floured surface and knead for 3-4 minutes until smooth. Form into a disk, wrap tightly in plastic wrap, and refrigerate fro 1 hour.
  4. Roll and cut: On a lightly floured surface, roll the dough out as thin as possible (approximately 6mm). Use a 10mm diameter round cookie cutter to cut out circles.
  5. Fold the shell: Brush one side of the dough circle with the reserved egg white. Roll the rough, egg white side down, around a greased cannoli tube and press the moistened edge firmly to seal.
  6. Fry the shells: Add 1-2 inches of vegetable oil to heavy-bottomed saucepan and heat to 185°C. Using tongs, carefully place a few shells into the hot oil and fry for 1-2 minutes, or until golden brown and blistered.
  7. Cool: Remove the fried shells and transfer to a paper towel-lined plate to drain. Once cool enough to handle, gently slide the shells out of the cannoli tubes.
